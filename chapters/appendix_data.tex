The electrolyser and fuel cell capacities are based on the individual countriesâ€™ national hydrogen strategies. All installed electrolyser and fuel cell capacities are listed in Table \ref{table:hydrogen_data}. If available, the national hydrogen strategies' expansion targets are used. If no capacity is specified, it is estimated based on predicted hydrogen demand. Further, 4000 full-load hours of electrolysers per year are assumed \cite{Kanellopoulos2019}. If no other targets exist, we expect capacity to double by 2040 compared to 2030. The fuel cell capacity equals 60\% of the electrolyser capacity. Each country's hydrogen storage units are not capacity-limited before the optimisation. The optimal required storage sizing is calculated ex-post in Appendix \ref{sec:appendix_hydrogen_storage}.
The electrolyser efficiency ($\eta_{e}$) is 70\% and the fuel cell efficiency ($\eta_{fc}$) is 60\%.

Austrias \ac{NECP} indicates that in balance, 100\% of the yearly electricity demand is compensated by \ac{RESE}; therefore, CO\textsubscript{2} emitting power plants are only used to a minor extent. The overall electricity demand of each country includes the progressing electrification of the coupled sectors heating, cooling and transport. The \ac{EV} demand according to the respective scenarios is subtracted from the \textit{\ac{NT} 2030} electricity demand considering this previously assumed \ac{EV} demand. Therefore, Austria's total electricity demand is identical to the \ac{ENTSOE} specifications. 


The \ac{SRMC}s are essential parameters to dispatch models with a significant impact on the resulting DA prices and the generation mix.
These parameters are calculated based on fuel costs, efficiency, emission factor, \ac{OM} costs and CO\textsubscript{2} price. Further, \ac{EV} driving patterns and the assumptions regarding their electricity demand are important as they influence overall flexibility potential.
